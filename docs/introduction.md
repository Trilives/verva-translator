# Introducing Verva Translate

> Carry the meaning. Keep the voice.
> 译出含义，也保留语气。

## Why it exists

Verva started as a personal itch. Machine translation often gets the words right while the context, warmth, precision, or the speaker's voice quietly goes missing — and I kept wanting a tool that let me say what a translation should *sound* like.

There is also the question of control. Many translators with genuinely useful AI features fold the model costs into a subscription. That is a reasonable business model, but it does not suit everyone: sometimes you would rather bring your own key, pick your own endpoint and model, and pay only for what you use.

Verva is built around three ideas:

1. Meaning matters, but tone and intent are part of the message too.
2. How a translation sounds should be something you can state directly.
3. The model, the endpoint, and the cost should be your choice.

It is a deliberately small Windows tool: a clean workspace, a handful of practical styles, direct compatible-API access, and a careful approach to storing your key locally. It does not translate without an AI model, and it does not hide model costs inside another subscription.

It is early software, and there is plenty still to improve. Bug reports and suggestions are genuinely welcome.

## 中文介绍

Verva 最初只是为了解决我自己的一个困扰。机器翻译常常字面无误，语境、温度、准确程度和说话者的语气却悄悄丢失了——我一直想要一个可以直接说明"译文该是什么语气"的工具。

另一个问题是选择权。不少具备实用 AI 能力的翻译工具会把模型成本并入订阅费。这本身是合理的商业模式，但并不适合所有人：有时你更希望自带密钥、自选端点和模型，只为实际用量付费。

Verva 建立在三个想法之上：

1. 含义很重要，但语气和意图同样属于信息的一部分。
2. 译文应该是什么语气，应该可以由你直接说明。
3. 模型、接口和调用成本，应该由你自己选择。

这是一款刻意做得很小的 Windows 工具：清晰的工作区、几种实用风格、直接的兼容接口调用，以及对本地密钥存储的谨慎处理。它不会在没有 AI 模型的情况下完成翻译，也不会把模型费用藏进另一项订阅里。

这仍是一个早期项目，还有很多需要改进的地方。欢迎提交问题反馈和建议。
